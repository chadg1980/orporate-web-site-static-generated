C. Passive Anonymous Information Gathered Electronically We and our third party partners may automatically collect certain types of passive, anonymous information whenever you use the Services, such as information about your device, your computer, and online activities. For instance, we may automatically collect Services usage information, such as the numbers and frequency of users of the Services, pages visited and similar data. This data is used in the aggregate, that is, as a statistical measure, and not in a manner that A to Z intends to use to identify you personally. If we collect this information, it will be done passively through the use of certain electronic technologies, such as cookies, web beacons, pixels, clear GIFs (and similar technologies) and location information from your wireless equipment as explained below. Cookies: We and our advertising partners may use “cookies.” When you use the Services, the pages you see, along with something called “cookies,” may be downloaded to your device or computer. Cookies are small pieces of information that are stored by your browser on your device or computer. These cookies do not contain or collect any PII, but may be used to deliver content specific to your interests, to control the number of times you see an advertisement while using the Services, to measure the effectiveness of advertising campaigns, to provide analytics concerning use of the Services, and for other purposes, such as security and administrative functions. For example, cookies are used to store your preferences for certain types of information so that you do not have to input those preferences every time you use the Services. Most web browsers automatically accept cookies, but you usually can change your browser to limit or prevent cookies. Cookies in themselves do not personally identify the individual user, but rather the computer or device that is being used to access our Services. You may have the opportunity to set your computer or device to accept all cookies, to notify you when a cookie is issued, or to not receive cookies at any time. Different browsers may address cookies differently. If you set your browser to not accept cookies, it may result in certain personalized services not being provided to you when you use that computer or device. Web beacons, Pixels and Clear GIFs: We and our advertising partners may use web beacons, pixels and clear GIFs. These electronic technologies are transparent image files that, if used, allow us and our advertising partners to track website usage information, such as the number of times a given web page has been viewed and whether and when you have opened a HTML email, how many times the email was forwarded and which links in the email were clicked. Unlike cookies, these technologies are not placed on your computer or device. This information helps us to improve our Services and helps our advertising partners by measuring the effectiveness of these communications with you. These technologies may be used in association with cookies to understand how visitors interact with our Service or advertisements made by our advertising partners.

Device Information: You can access and use the Services via different types of devices, including computers, tablets and smartphones. Depending on the device used, we and our third party vendors may collect certain device information. For instance, in order to provide analytics concerning the Services, the following information may be collected: hardware and software types, browser information, IP address, and device identifiers. When you use the Services on a wireless device, including your own device or the HealthSlate tablet, the Services may provide additional functionality by using the location of your wireless device (including the city, state and/or country where you were located when you download a mobile application.
